06 ——— Groupwork

Designing for a scaffolding exercise and facilitating a tinkering workshop

Scaffolding is fun. How to scaffold for a workshop?

This project was the group project in the course, but designing for a tinkering workshop with scaffolding, also required a lot of scaffolding in the project too.

*image generated with ChatGPT
01 Context & Initial Idea
The original idea

At first, we started with a cardsort version of the StoryCubes, because of their variability. Inspired by games that create way different stories, based on their prompt order.
The second idea was about learning to code with the Raspberry Pico. The project would be about a novel way of displaying/telling the time.

The End

Because of the limitation of the Pico, the clock-project wouldn't suffice. The other idea however, implemented a new way of telling stories with shadows ánd a Pico. This would then provide autonomous movements to let the shadows move.

02 Idea & Design

First, we started with a small brainstorm session, what will the session be about?

Step 2: Developing an Idea

Presentation should include:
(framing, scope) idea, problem space, goal formulation (what type of outcome?), technical hurdles to solve
(materials) seed, prompt, toolbox
(scaffolding, facilitation) - what do you need, which on-boarding?
(process) location, space

Idea:
Let all students bring their own "thing," which they have to incorporate in their project, so there is an element of surprise.
The facilitator should provide some liquids



What is a good prompt (from the book):
1. Brevity (it should be "clear, concise, and self-evident")
2. Ambiguity (the learner should be free in making choices, this could lead to surprising elements!)
3. Immunity to assessment. Our prompt: Design a shadow-based storytelling experience
An experience could be: a real life performance, a video, a stop motion, a collection of pictures, etc. (but do not explain this to students - leave it open)

Pitch 2

The storytelling experience should be more tangible, more applicated into a context, as creativeness cannot emerge from no boundaries. After the first pitch, the focus was more on the outcome of the stories: we chose for shadow stories, since it is at the beginning of the human existence, that it almost feels natural. It is a universal languange that everyone speaks, so it is very applicable to a multinational create students.

04 Physical Realization & Interoperability

As the idea for this workshop more took shape, we iterated on the technical hurdles and the operability of the execution of the workshop. What should be facilitated? What should be part of the projectwork for the students?

Conclusion: at first we had the shadow experience as an open undefined subject. Working with this box-setup felt like more of a technical hurdle than exploring a shadow-based storytelling experience. Hence, we opted for a white wall, provided by the facilitator from the beginning.

Personal note: defining the intended result display (shadows on the white wall) was a rather big design choice in terms of vision. Personally, it felt quite contradicting to I think it is hard to define what should be in- and excluded in a prompt for scaffolding. Creativeness is only creative to that context. For me that was quite a turning point in the project, as it became easier to define other boundaries for this project.

Soon, defining parts of our workshop made it clearer what the end result should be more tailored toward. If the rest is defined, the participants will be able to move freely around the story, and not think about the peviously undefined hurdles.

... and finally...!

The flashlight! Some flashlights were brighter than the others, meaning the light didn't create strong enough shadows. Also, wider lights didn't really work, as they contain multiple led lights. These multiple leds create a diffuse shadow, undesired for this project.

As the first idea was about throwing people in the deep sea, the workshop slowly began to take shape and more tangible on what the facilitator should provide.

Most Surprising Element

For me, the most surprising element was that shadows in 2D can have a completely different origin object. The origin object can be completely ugly, taped together, cut wrongly or even have a lot of dust on it: all of it is not in the shadow. It is just an outline of the product in the solid form. The second surprising part for me was that the easiest modifyable materials for this workshop are "knutsel spullen". these objects are easy modifyable, to create the desired story. However, the orginal idea was to create shadows from random objects, found in the participants bags. With that vision in mind, the storycubes would make it a bit more complicated, as that would call for characters or shadows that can't be generated by these objects from participant's bags. This creates a conflict of the sustainability idea and reversible effect of objects. The objects made with "knutsel spullen" will be thown away after, while the 'backpack objects' would be used for their intended use again.

This question makes me wonder how the workshop could enhance both principles: the story cubes and the reusable object. Or should the project completely have another vision if this reversible effect has the priority...?
